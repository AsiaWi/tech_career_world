Could you tell me about your current career: what is it you do now? What does a typical day look like for you? How does your work involve technology, digital and data?

Day to day, I work on data/digital product development projects to investigate and understand business needs, current business architecture, strategies and target models to help shape the future strategic models for the business area in which the new product will sit. This involves lots of stakeholder engagements, analysis of models/documents, running workshops, producing business models, working with technical colleagues to draw up options, working with User Researchers to understand user needs, advising seniors and making recommendations.

Have any of your previous roles involved technology, digital and data, including outside the civil service? If so can you give me a bit of information about what they were and what was involved please.

I have worked in the civil service for over 12 years in various departments in operational delivery and policy roles. Recently I moved to a more technical role working as a Senior Business Analyst.

Why did you choose a career in technology?

I wanted to explore the opportunties and different professions on offer in digital and technology.

If you had to give advice to students deciding whether to choose a career in technology, what would you say to them?

You don't need to make any decisions now about your career, go try lots of different things and think about what bits you enjoy, then explore the routes available to do the things you enjoy. Find ways to keep learning after you leave education, some things you will enjoy, some you won't, they are all clues to help direct you forward. No matter what career you choose, the skills that will help you the most and lead you to success are communicating well, taking the time to understand people, being brave and curious to try new things.

Are there any particular people who have inspired you in your career?

My mentors, women I've been on career development programmes with, Brene Brown on leadership.

Have you done any kinds of technical qualification e.g. GCSEs, a degree, an apprenticeship, etc? Would you recommend them and why?

I have a BSc Combined STEM which was focused on Science rather than being technical. I didn't require any qualifications for any of the roles I've done in the civil service including my current role. I have obtained and am working towards some professional qualifications for my current role.
